GAME DESCRIPTION

YEAR: 1980
DEVELOPER: Atari, Inc.
DESIGNER: Dave Theurer

The world is under attack! Six cities are being bombarded by an endless barrage of nuclear missiles. As the commander of three anti-missile batteries, you are the last line of defense.

Missile Command is a fixed shooter arcade game. It features a unique trackball control scheme and a strategic element where players must manage limited ammunition while protecting multiple targets. MISSION BRIEFING

1. THE THREAT: Enemy ICBMs are launched from the top of the screen. They travel in arcs toward your cities or your batteries.

2. INTERCEPTORS: Use the trackball (or mouse) to aim your crosshair. Press the fire button to launch an interceptor missile from your battery.

3. EXPLOSIONS: The interceptor does not hit the enemy directly. It creates a large explosion cloud. If an enemy missile passes through this cloud, it is destroyed.

4. SMART BOMBS: Later levels introduce MIRVs (Multiple Re-entry Vehicles) and Satellites. MIRVs split into multiple warheads. Smart Bombs create massive explosions but are rare.

5. GAME OVER: If all 6 cities are destroyed, the game ends. Survive as long as possible!
